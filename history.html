Before Transportation

Before the paved roads, people travel by foot or on mule- or horseback until 1961 and Bhutan was a roadless country or inhabited wilderness until 1962, when the first road was constructed from the Indian border town of Phuentsholing to Paro. The first 175-kilometre-long (109 mile) paved road was completed in 1962. About 30,000 Indian and Nepalese labourers were imported for the construction work.

Dantak was primarily tasked with constructing motorable roads in Bhutan. In 1968, it completed the road connecting Samdrup Jongkhar to Trashigang, and in the same year, Thimphu was connected to Phuentsholing by Dantak. Built in 1968 as an airstrip for on-call helicopter services for the Indian Armed Forces. Now used as an international airport. These pioneering projects were essential in connecting the remote areas of Bhutan to the rest of the world, promoting trade and commerce, and improving the living standards of people living in the region.
